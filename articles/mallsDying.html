Why Malls Are Dying

By Tyler Trinidad on 8/14/19


Footlocker, Champs, Footaction, Shoe Palace. These stores are staples for the average mall. They used to be a place where a person can buy the latest fashion and it was the spot where everyone came during raffles and releases. Now those days are numbered. In 2018, Forbes reported that 110 footwear stores have been closed down and it will continue to grow.


**2 reasons why I believe that malls are dying


1) StockX, GOAT, ETC… Online Sneaker Shopping


These online platforms have done something insane. They have made the hunt for grails extremely easy. If a shoe is sold out they have it (resale price though), but in general these shoe sites make it so pretty much anyone can purchase.

That being said, this completely destroys the search for shoes which in part was one of the best parts about looking for a shoe. Going from mall to mall, store to store, and seeing if Champs, Footlockers, or Shoe Palace had a retro Jordan 8 was just a great part of the chase for the drip.


2) The Stores Suck


This is part the stores fault and part the producers fault. Nike, Adidas and even Puma really don’t give these stores any heat. Its mostly just GR's and then a lot of trash (TEAM JORDAN ☹ haha) on the shelves. This cant be helping these stores epsecially when it comes to the large amount of Hypebeasts walking around as this potentially decreases the amount of revenue they could bring in.

But seriously, unless its drop day, are people looking for more then just Prestos and NMDs.


I believe overall though this can be fixed. Footlocker, for example, has begun a youth power movement. It bought GOAT and now I can tell its trying to change its branding. Hopefully we will see if it works out.
